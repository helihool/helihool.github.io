If you ever suffered from depression, anxiety or loneliness ‚Äì I am sorry. I truly am. I myself suffered from depression during my bachelor studies and had to take a year off to improve my health. However, being involved in community music projects, helped me to get back on track. Read this article and see if this might be a way to get better for you.

Mental health

There are now a few studies that were conducted amongst professional musicians on mental health. Recent one showed that more than 73% of independent music makers suffer from symptoms of mental illness (Record Union, 2019). Another pilot survey from a few years ago revealed that of 2,211 self-identifying professional musicians 71.1% of respondents believed they had experienced incidences of anxiety and panic attacks, 68.5% of respondents experienced incidences of depression (Help Musicians UK, 2014).

Many musical students I know have suffered from mild to severe depression. Some of them quit their studies, unable to cope with competitiveness, pressure to please and financial instability that beginning of the musical career is full of. Others, continued and found ways to overcome the problems with the help of professionals. Those, who had fortune to experience the benefits from participating in community music projects ‚Äì continued on this journey.

In The Impact of Community Engagement for Professional Musicians author of the research Sara Ascenso points out that ‚Äúengaging musically with communities contributes to the musicians‚Äô wellbeing‚Äù (Ascenso, 2016).

This happens through the experience of positive emotions, and also by boosting a sense of accomplishment, purpose and through cultivating social connections. It also bring a much-needed variety to musicians‚Äô daily routine, allowing for greater engagement and excitement about music-making.

Note from the author

Being involved in music-making for a community, freed my creative spirit from a competitive mindset that I, similar to many who pursue musical career, developed over the years.

When one can craft its art in a friendly environment and at the same time has a possibility to see how this art can be helpful and healing for others, one sees a great purpose and meaning in continuing to pursue a musical career.
